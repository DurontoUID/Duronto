I made the mistake of reading about how to set this up to work with Android TV (Google), before trying just because I was not sure where my TV manual was. For the life of me I could not find the setting on my TV to set up a peripheral device besides Bluetooth, which this device doesn't require as it has its own dongle. Now maybe this was for a prior iteration of Smart TVs hence why I could not even find what they were referencing. So I decided to ignore those websites and treat my TV like any other Android device. Plug in the dongle, make sure the controller is set to Android, which according to the manual is when the lighted buttons 3 and 4 (bottom two lights of the home button) are active. Just hold the home button for 5 seconds each time to change the setting.


Well, after the controller was in Android mode, I accidently touched a button on the controller and noticed my TV responding to button clicks. I assumed that the controller was now correctly paired and decided to test a game. I did check the settings on the game first using the TV remote to select the input for the game (one game had to change from touch to button, others might say controller) and voila! Turned out to be as easy as pie as with other Android devices! I don't know why some people have to complicate things by talking about game emulators and peripheral inputs. My experience turned out to be plug and play like PC. If the game is not compatible, that's when all the other advise might come into play.
